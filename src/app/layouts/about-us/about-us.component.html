What is ESG Report?

ESG reporting is the disclosure of information about the environment, society, and corporate governance. Its goal is to bring attention to a company's ESG operations, enhance investor transparency, and encourage other businesses to follow suit.

What do ESG reports include?

ESG reports contain both qualitative and quantitative data about its three main subjects.

Environmental:

How an organization is addressing climate change How an organization is cutting carbon emissions How the organization is preventing deforestation, enhancing air and water quality, protecting biodiversity, or properly managing its waste How the organization manages its supply chain and uses resources in an ethical manner What steps is the organization taking to lower its emissions?

Social:

How an organization cares for its employees and workplace Initiatives promoting gender, BIPOC, and LGBTQ+ inclusivity Engagement of employees at the company preserving the privacy of data Community participation Employment laws and labour standards

Governance:

The internal controls of a corporation Leadership, board composition, executive compensation, audit committee structure, shareholder rights, bribery, lobbying, political contributions, and whistle-blower programs policies, principles, and procedures
